About TechnoVision

Empowering Future Tech Leaders

TechnoVision is the official technology club of Sanskriti University, established in 2023 with the vision to create a platform for students to explore, innovate, and excel in various technological domains.

Our club brings together students from all disciplines who share a common passion for technology. We organize regular workshops, hackathons, coding competitions, and tech talks featuring industry experts.

With over 500 active members, we've won numerous national-level competitions and have collaborated with tech giants like Google, Microsoft, and Amazon for various initiatives.

Federated Learning for Healthcare Data Privacy

Our team is developing novel federated learning architectures that enable collaborative machine learning across hospitals without sharing sensitive patient data. This approach maintains data privacy while improving diagnostic AI models.

Current results show 15% improvement in pneumonia detection from X-rays compared to centralized models, with zero raw data exchange between institutions.

Team: 6 researchers Ongoing since Jan 2022 2 papers published

Explainable AI for Financial Fraud Detection

We're creating interpretable machine learning models that not only detect fraudulent transactions but also provide human-understandable explanations for their decisions, addressing the "black box" problem in financial AI systems.

Our SHAP-based approach achieves 92% accuracy while reducing false positives by 30% compared to traditional deep learning models.

Team: 4 researchers Ongoing since Aug 2021 Patent pending

Quantum Machine Learning Algorithms

Exploring hybrid quantum-classical neural networks that leverage quantum parallelism for specific subroutines in deep learning. Our initial tests show potential for exponential speedup in certain optimization problems.

Current work focuses on implementing quantum versions of backpropagation and gradient descent on IBM's quantum computers.

Team: 5 researchers Ongoing since Mar 2023 1 paper in review

Post-Quantum Cryptography

Developing lattice-based cryptographic algorithms resistant to quantum computer attacks. Our team is working on optimizing these algorithms for practical implementation in IoT devices with limited computational resources.

Current benchmarks show our approach uses 40% less memory than standard lattice cryptography implementations.

Team: 3 researchers Ongoing since Oct 2022 Collaboration with DRDO

Blockchain for Supply Chain Transparency

Implementing Hyperledger Fabric-based solutions for agricultural supply chains to ensure product authenticity from farm to consumer. Our system reduces paperwork by 70% while improving traceability.

Pilot project with local organic farmers shows 95% reduction in counterfeit products entering the supply chain.

Team: 7 researchers Ongoing since Jun 2021 Industry partnership

Energy-Efficient Consensus Mechanisms

Developing novel Proof-of-Stake variants that reduce blockchain energy consumption by 99% compared to Bitcoin-style Proof-of-Work, while maintaining security against Sybil attacks.

Our "Proof-of-Reputation" algorithm incorporates node history and community trust metrics to achieve consensus with minimal energy expenditure.

Team: 4 researchers Ongoing since Feb 2023 2 conference presentations

Edge AI for Smart Agriculture

Deploying lightweight neural networks on microcontroller-based edge devices to analyze soil conditions and crop health in real-time. Our system processes data locally, reducing cloud dependency and bandwidth requirements by 90%.

Field tests show 30% reduction in water usage while maintaining crop yields through precise irrigation control.

Team: 5 researchers Ongoing since Sep 2022 Government grant funded

Federated Learning for IoT Security

Creating distributed anomaly detection systems where IoT devices collaboratively learn to identify cyber threats without sharing raw sensor data. Our approach improves detection rates while preserving privacy.

Initial results show 85% accuracy in identifying novel attack patterns across smart home devices.

Team: 6 researchers Ongoing since Jan 2023 Partnership with Cisco
